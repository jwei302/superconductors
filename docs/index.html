Crystal structure diffusion

t = —

All three crystal components are denoised jointly by one E(3)-equivariant graph network:

  • Fractional coordinates — diffused on the periodic 3-torus via wrapped-normal score matching, sampled with a predictor–corrector (Langevin) scheme that respects translational symmetry.
  • Atom types — each atom is relaxed to a continuous 100-dimensional one-hot vector, diffused with Gaussian DDPM, then decoded back to a discrete element by arg-max.
  • Lattice (3×3) — the lattice matrix is diffused with Gaussian DDPM, equivariant to global rotations of the unit cell.

GRPO post-training

The pre-trained generator is then fine-tuned with Group Relative Policy Optimization. For a target Tc, the policy samples a group of candidate crystals; each is scored by a BEE-NET Allen–Dynes Tc model; the policy is pushed toward the members that beat the group average.

Real crystals generated for a target Tc and scored by BEE-NET — one GRPO group. Candidates above the group mean are reinforced, below are suppressed; repeated over training, this pulls the generator toward higher-Tc structures. Drag to rotate any cell.

VPO — vector (multi-objective) policy optimization

GRPO optimizes one scalar reward. VPO (Bahlous-Boldi et al., 2026) optimizes a vector reward so the sampled group spreads across the Pareto front — raising best-of-k hit rate under downstream screens. Here the vector is three complementary objectives, all scored in one torch process (no TensorFlow):
